Soft robotic wearable system for ankle sprain prevention

I have been working as an undergraduate researcher at Biorobotics Laboratory since 2020 (Advised by Kyujin Cho). I developed a soft robotic wearable system for ankle sprain prevention, where I oversaw the overall development of the system: design, fabrication, and system integration.


The device places Active I/E Support Units on either side of the ankle to increase the INV/EV stiffness of the ankle.
The unit has two features; 1) anisotropic structural characteristic for enhancing INV/EV stiffness with minimal effect on DF/PF stiffness which allows minimal interference with user’s natural walking, and 2) pneumatic-based active engaging mechanism where the level of ankle assistance can be controlled by controlling the pneumatic pressure inside the units.

The RGB-D camera mounted on the user’s waist acquires real-time images of the ground and both legs, and the control algorithm determines the ground roughness and the gait phase based on these images. Based on these information, the assistance level of the device is controlled in order to adjust to various walking environments.


I am currently working on further experiments with additional subjects for a more rigorous evaluation of the ankle wearable system.
